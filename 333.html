OVERVIEW

The team project was completed as a final project for Interaction Design Methods course. The goal of this project was to explore various research methods by conducting extensive research on Port Moody Ecological Society’s Water Quality Team. The Water Quality Team’s goal is to maintain the water quality of the creeks in the Burrard Inlet, ensuring a suitable environment for the salmon.

PROCESS

Ethnography

The tasks of the Water Quality Team can be categorized into two components – fieldwork and laboratory work. Throughout the process working with the Water Quality team members, we found out that there two main potentials that we could have further investigation. Firstly, there are safety risks in the field as we had to climb down steep, slippery and muddy hills to acess creeks and collect water samples. Secondly, during the water sample analysis process, we can esaily forget the analysis we are conducting or misplaced the samples by accident as the tasks are repetitive and especially when we are talking to each other.

See Result

Cultural Probes

Another research method we used was creating cultural probes – artifacts that were intend to reveal certain aspects about our participants (emotions, behaviours, values, thoughts, etc.) in a fun and engaging way. Hence, we created hand-made unique cultural probes specifically targeting the hatchery's water quality team member. Our cultural probe kit include: sacred map of the creeks, limited edition game cards, DIY pop can, legendary self-expression box, newsflash tomorrow (a newspaper article), and message in hidden eggs. From the interviews’and probes’ data, we and inferred about our participants’ personal behaviour and attitudes and discover some unexpected ideas that the participants pointed out.

See Result

Persona

As we conducted a series of research methods and informal interviews, we had found valuable information which allowed us to develop personas. These personas helped us to shape our main target user and offer an inexpensive way to test and prioritize features throughout the development.

See Result

Participatory Workshop

The last research method was to hold a participatory workshop for the members of the Water Quality. The activities of the workshop were designed to discover members' deeper needs and wants in a fun and interactive way. We conduct three activities: a mad lib, building your dream laboratory and designing your ideal tools.

See Result

Storyboard & Journey Framework

According to the personas that we set at the previous stage, we also created a storyboard and journey framework to describe the members’ emotions flow and new experience in our new laboratory.

See Result

RESULT

Design Solution

Our design solution for the Water Quality Team was designing a new laboratory that would speed up their workflow. We created a 3D model prototype to show how their workflow can be improved.
